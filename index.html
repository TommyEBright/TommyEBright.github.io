Latest posters.

Two posters presented at the International Universities Strength and Conditioning Association (IUSCA) conference, 2026.

Poster 1 · IUSCA 2026
Differences in Braking Phase Mechanics Between Loaded and Accentuated Eccentric Countermovement Jumps
Bridgeman LA, Ellis M, Puleston CM, Suchomel TJ, Mitchell LN, Sinclair L, Sharland J, Bright TE

CMJ_AEL and CMJ_L produce distinctly different braking phase characteristics. AEL increases rate of force application; external loading increases force at the braking endpoint.

View poster (PDF)
Poster 2 · IUSCA 2026
Effects of Accentuated Eccentric and External Loading on Countermovement Jump Landing Performance
Sinclair L, Ellis M, Puleston CM, Harry J, Willis J, Mitchell LN, Pead O, Bright TE

AEL maintains landing performance across 20–40% body mass loads, whereas traditional external loading of equivalent magnitude does not. AEL is more appropriate where landing quality is a priority.

View poster (PDF)

About

Who's behind this.

This site is maintained by Dr. Tommy Bright and Dr. Matt Ellis at Plymouth Marjon University. Tommy holds a PhD in Sports Biomechanics and has extensive applied and research experience in biomechanics and strength and conditioning across university, academy, and elite sport settings.

The teaching content here is designed to be used alongside — not instead of — your lectures and workshops. Use the interactive tools to explore concepts, the quizzes to test your understanding, and the reading lists to go deeper.
