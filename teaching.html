Teaching & Learning

Biomechanics
from the ground up.

Resources, tools, and interactive simulations for sport science students at every level — built around understanding movement before measuring it.

Approach

Why before how.

Good biomechanics teaching starts with observation, not numbers. Drawing on Duane Knudson's qualitative analysis framework, students here develop the ability to watch a movement and ask the right questions before they reach for a force plate.

Resources are designed to build understanding progressively. Level 4 students explore mechanics visually and interactively. By Masters level, they are working with real data, real uncertainty, and real practitioner decisions.

Every tool here is designed to make the underlying mechanics visible — not to replace thinking, but to provoke it.

"The purpose of qualitative analysis is to improve performance through systematic observation and evaluation."
Duane Knudson — Fundamentals of Biomechanics
