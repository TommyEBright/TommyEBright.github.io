Upload & analyse your CMJ trial

Drop a force-plate CSV (sampled at 1000 Hz; vertical GRF in Newtons) and the app will detect phases, compute the full set of force, velocity, displacement, power and RFD variables, and plot the trace with landmarks. The first ~1 s of the file is used as quiet standing to compute body weight.

Detection notes

Onset of movement: force leaves a window of BW ± 5 × SD(BW), then the algorithm steps back 30 samples to capture true onset. Take-off and touchdown use a noise-derived threshold (5 × SD of the flight-phase tail). Frame numbers and times are reported in the methods row of the output.

Upload & analyse an IMTP trial

Drop a force-plate CSV from an isometric mid-thigh pull (sampled at 1000 Hz; vertical GRF in Newtons). The first ~1 s of the file is treated as quiet standing on the plate to compute system weight. The app reports peak force, time-windowed RFD, force at fixed time intervals, and net impulse.

Trial setup

Click or drop a CSV here

Single column of force (N), or time + force columns. Header row optional.

Detection notes

The athlete stands on the plate gripping the bar — that's the system weight (body + bar). Pull onset is detected as the first sample where force exceeds system weight + 5 × SD. Time windows for RFD (50, 100, 150, 200, 250 ms) are measured from onset.
