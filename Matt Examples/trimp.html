How it works

A reference for the formulas and file formats used by this app.

Workflow
  1. Set up each player once on the Roster tab. Enter their constants from the lab test.
  2. For every session: Session tab → pick the player → upload the HR CSV → all five TRIMPs are calculated.
  3. Constants stay saved on this device permanently. Use Export to back up or move to another device.
The five TRIMPs

ΔHR ratio = (HR − HRrest) / (HRmax − HRrest), computed per sample.

iTRIMP (Manzi): sum over samples of dt · ΔHR · A · eB·ΔHR. Uses each player's individual exponential.

bTRIMP (Banister): duration · ΔHRmean · 0.64 · e1.92·ΔHRmean. Male generic coefficients.

eTRIMP (Edwards): time-in-zone × zone weighting (1–5). Zones at 50/60/70/80/90% HRmax.

luTRIMP (Lucia): time-in-zone × weighting (1–3). Zone 1 < HRLT; Zone 2 between LT and OBLA; Zone 3 > HROBLA.

sRPE (Foster): duration (min) × RPE (0–10).

CSV format

The app auto-detects the layout. Accepted shapes:

  • Single column of HR: one bpm value per row. Sampling rate from the dropdown.
  • Two columns: time (s) and HR. Header row optional. Time can be in seconds or h:mm:ss.
  • Common HR column names recognised: HR, Heart rate, bpm, HeartRate.

Decimal separator must be a dot. Anything that isn't a finite number ≥ 30 and ≤ 240 is treated as missing and skipped.

Privacy

Everything runs in your browser. Files never leave this device — not to a server, not to me. Closing the tab doesn't lose your roster (it's saved in local storage). Clearing browser data will erase it, so export a backup if it matters.
