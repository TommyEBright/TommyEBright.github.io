Purpose

Why collaborate? Three principles guide this research programme:

Increase sample size

Collaborating across institutions allows us to recruit larger, more representative samples than any single university could achieve alone.

Knowledge exchange

Sharing expertise, methods and ideas across partner universities to strengthen the quality and reach of our research.

Student centred

Putting students at the heart of the research process, offering opportunities to contribute to and learn from real, ongoing projects.

Current research projects

A selection of ongoing research projects exploring different aspects of sport science.

Force-platform study

AEL Project

The study investigates how athletes — both youth (11–18) and adults (18–35) — tolerate and use eccentric momentum during loaded and accentuated eccentric load (AEL) countermovement jumps, using force-platform data to examine braking strategies, take-off momentum, and jump height across loads of 10–50% body mass.

Youth athletes · CMJ

G&M Project

The study investigates how growth and maturation influence countermovement jump (CMJ) performance in youth athletes, using force-platform waveform analysis to examine how force–time, velocity, and power profiles evolve across maturity stages rather than relying solely on discrete variables such as jump height or peak force.
