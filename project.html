About

What is this project?

This project brings students into real research — not as assistants, but as contributors. Whether you're an undergraduate collecting force plate data or a postgraduate running statistical analyses, your work here feeds into published outputs.

The project spans multiple strands aligned with the lab's core themes: countermovement jump analysis, athlete monitoring, and strength and conditioning practice.

Contributors get real research experience, authorship credit where appropriate, and a stronger CV for postgraduate study or applied practice.

Get involved
Who can get involved

Undergraduate students (L5–6), postgraduate students, and PhD candidates. No prior research experience needed for data collection roles.

What's involved

Force plate testing, literature reviewing, statistical analysis in R or MATLAB, and manuscript preparation — depending on your level and interests.

What you get

Research experience, authorship credit, reference letters, and a meaningful addition to your CV or postgraduate application.
